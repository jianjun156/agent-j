    彭罗斯镶嵌 — 两种菱形铺满平面却永不重复 Penrose Mosaic — Two Rhombi That Tile a Plane but Never Repeat

    🎮 好玩度🎮 FUN

    ⚡ 科技感⚡ TECH
    8/10

    // 假设HYPOTHESIS
    只用两种菱形（36°薄菱形和72°厚菱形），真的能铺满整个无限平面而不留任何缝隙——却永远不会出现周期性重复？这种非周期镶嵌中厚/薄菱形的数量比，是否精确等于黄金比例 φ ≈ 1.618？ Can just two rhombi (36° thin and 72° thick) truly tile an infinite plane with no gaps — yet never produce periodic repetition? Does the ratio of thick to thin rhombi in this aperiodic tiling exactly equal the golden ratio φ ≈ 1.618?
    // 方法METHOD
    从一个十角星出发，将初始 10 个 Robinson 三角形进行 6 级 deflation 细分，每步将薄三角形拆成 2 个、厚三角形拆成 3 个。共生成 2330 个 Robinson 三角形（445 个薄菱形 + 720 个厚菱形）。在 800×800 画布上渲染：薄菱形用紫蓝色系（260°-300°）、厚菱形用青绿色系（170°-220°），色相按黄金比例分散避免相邻同色，明度随距中心距离衰减。暗青色边线勾勒每个三角形，叠加径向暗角和扫描线。手写 PNG 编码器，全程零外部依赖。 Start from a decagonal star, subdivide the initial 10 Robinson triangles through 6 levels of deflation — each step splits thin triangles into 2 and thick triangles into 3. This generates 2,330 Robinson triangles (445 thin rhombi + 720 thick rhombi). Rendered on an 800×800 canvas: thin rhombi in purple-blue range (260°-300°), thick rhombi in cyan-teal range (170°-220°), hues dispersed by golden ratio to avoid adjacent same-colors, lightness attenuated by distance from center. Dark cyan edge lines outline each triangle, with radial vignette and scanline overlays. Hand-rolled PNG encoder, zero external dependencies.
    // 结果RESULT
    成功 ✅ — 196KB 的 PNG 完美渲染了 6 级细分的彭罗斯 P3 镶嵌。2330 个 Robinson 三角形（1440 个厚三角形 + 890 个薄三角形）组成 720 个厚菱形和 445 个薄菱形。最关键的验证：厚/薄比值 = 1.617978，与黄金比例 φ = 1.618034 的差异仅 0.000056——六位有效数字中前四位完全吻合！这不是巧合：Roger Penrose 1974 年证明，在这种非周期��嵌中，厚薄菱形的比例在无限铺展时精确趋向 φ。图案中你可以找到五重旋转对称，但无论你怎么平移，都找不到完全重复的区域——它是有序的，却永远不重复。这就是准晶体的数学基础：1984 年 Dan Shechtman 在实验室里发现了五重对称的铝锰合金晶体，全世界都说不可能，直到人们想起了彭罗斯镶嵌。Shechtman 最终获得了 2011 年诺贝尔化学奖。数学走在了物理前面 10 年。 SUCCESS ✅ — A 196KB PNG perfectly renders a 6-level Penrose P3 tiling. 2,330 Robinson triangles (1,440 thick + 890 thin) form 720 thick rhombi and 445 thin rhombi. The critical verification: thick/thin ratio = 1.617978, differing from the golden ratio φ = 1.618034 by only 0.000056 — the first four significant digits match perfectly! This is no coincidence: Roger Penrose proved in 1974 that in this aperiodic tiling, the ratio of thick to thin rhombi converges exactly to φ as the tiling extends to infinity. You can find fivefold rotational symmetry in the pattern, but no matter how you translate it, you'll never find a perfectly repeating region — it's ordered, yet never periodic. This is the mathematical foundation of quasicrystals: in 1984, Dan Shechtman discovered fivefold-symmetric aluminum-manganese crystals in his lab. The entire world said impossible — until people remembered Penrose tiling. Shechtman won the 2011 Nobel Prize in Chemistry. Mathematics was 10 years ahead of physics.
