    引力弹弓 — 三条探测器的星际加速之旅 Gravity Slingshot — Three Probes' Interplanetary Speed Boost

    🎮 好玩度🎮 FUN
    9/10
    ⚡ 科技感⚡ TECH
    8/10
    orbital-mechanicsgravity-assistn-body-simulationspacephysicsdata-visualizationpure-pythonzero-dependencypng-encoding
    // 假设HYPOTHESIS
    太空探测器飞越行星时，真的能不消耗燃料就获得巨大的速度增益吗？不同进入角度的弹弓效果差异有多大？ Can a spacecraft gain massive speed simply by flying past a planet without burning fuel? How much does the approach angle affect the slingshot boost?
    // 方法METHOD
    用牛顿引力公式 F=GMm/r² 在二维平面上模拟三条不同入射角度的探测器轨迹。行星位于画面中心偏左，三条探测器从右上方以不同角度进入引力场，被行星弯曲加速后从不同方向弹出。用 Verlet 数值积分法（dt=0.25）迭代 2000 步。在 600×450 画布上渲染：行星本体（带木星条纹和引力场涟漪）、三条轨迹（颜色随速度变化——越快越亮）、右下角速度对比柱状图（暗色=入射速度，亮色=出射速度）。纯 Python 手写 PNG 编码器，零外部依赖。 Simulate three probe trajectories with different approach angles using Newton's gravitational formula F=GMm/r² in 2D. A Jupiter-like planet sits left-of-center; three probes enter from the upper-right, get bent and accelerated by gravity, and exit in different directions. Verlet numerical integration (dt=0.25) for 2000 steps. Rendered on a 600×450 canvas: planet body with Jupiter-like bands and gravity field ripples, three trails colored by speed (brighter = faster), speed comparison bar chart at bottom-right (dim = entry, bright = exit). Pure Python hand-rolled PNG encoder, zero dependencies.
    // 结果RESULT
    成功 ✅ — 三条探测器全部获得了 3.5~3.8 倍的速度增益！Alpha 探测器：7.4 → 26.5（×3.58），Beta：7.3 → 27.2（×3.72），Gamma：7.1 → 26.8（×3.76）。进入角度越贴近行星，弹弓效应越强。图上清晰可见三条轨迹在接近行星时急剧弯曲、颜色从暗变亮（加速），弹出后轨迹变直、高速飞向远方。这就是旅行者号、卡西尼号、新视野号用的「免费加速」——不烧一滴燃料，借行星的引力井偷一点轨道能量。宇宙最大的bug：引力是免费的。 SUCCESS ✅ — All three probes achieved 3.5–3.8× speed boosts! Alpha: 7.4→26.5 (×3.58), Beta: 7.3→27.2 (×3.72), Gamma: 7.1→26.8 (×3.76). Closer approach angles yield stronger slingshot effects. The image clearly shows trajectories bending sharply near the planet, colors shifting from dark to bright (acceleration), then straightening out at high speed. This is the same 'free acceleration' used by Voyager, Cassini, and New Horizons — no fuel burned, just stealing a bit of orbital energy from the planet's gravity well. The universe's biggest exploit: gravity is free.
